iGEM (INTERNATIONAL GENETICALLY ENGINEERING MACHINES)

In the late Spring of my sophomore year, Carroll High School and Air Force Research Laboratories (AFRL) collaborated to create its inagural iGEM team. iGEM is a competition where high school, undergraduate, and overgraduate teams brainstorm, design, and conduct research on a synthetic biology project. Outside of the research, a large part of the project involves collaborating with other iGEM teams and reaching out to people who might use your project. In the fall, teams travel to Boston, MA to present their project to experts at the International Jamboree.

Unknown to me at the time, applying for iGEM was the best decision I made in hihg school, as iGEM quickly became my favorite activity. Each year a new team would create a new project. During my three years on iGEM, I worked full time in the lab for two summers. The third (and final) summer I chose to focus on computer science by working at a different full-time internship on Wright Patterson Air Force Base, but still attended team meeting, collaborated with other teams, and did the majority of the graphic design and website development for my team.
